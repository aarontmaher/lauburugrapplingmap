# BLACK BELT SYLLABUS
Mastery. Complete positional understanding across the full map.
Started: 2026-03-25

## Principles
- Complete game — can attack, defend, and transition from every mapped position
- Personal system mastery — the student's own game is fully developed
- Teaching capability — can explain and demonstrate every position
- Competition readiness — game plan creation and opponent preparation
- No-gi only
- Tied to actual positions in the map
- ALL technique selections from Aaron's mapped content only

## Promotion Rule
To earn Black belt status, the student must:
- Demonstrate complete positional mastery across the full map
- Show a fully developed personal game with live training proof
- Demonstrate the ability to teach/explain every section
- Provide video proof of mastery-level competition or high-level sparring
- No exceptions

## Prerequisite
Brown belt syllabus must be completed with video proof.

---

## 1. Complete Positional Mastery
The student must demonstrate competence in every canonical position on the map.

### Dominant Positions (all 6)
- Front Headlock — full system (attack + defence + transitions)
- Side Control — full system
- Mount — full system
- Back Control — full system
- Turtle — full system (attacker + defender)
- North South — full system

### Guard Positions (demonstrate from at least 10 of 19)
Student chooses their primary guards and demonstrates mastery:
- At least 3 closed/half guard variants
- At least 3 open guard variants
- At least 2 leg entanglement positions
- At least 2 seated/standing guard positions

### Wrestling (all core positions)
- All 3 White belt hand-fighting positions with full chain depth
- Shot offence and defence with counter-to-counter
- Clinch-to-ground mastery

### Scrambles (all 3)
- Berimbolo — full system
- Crab ride — full system
- Grounded 50/50 — full system

## 2. Personal Game Development
The student has a fully developed personal game.

### A-Game
- Clear primary positions identified
- 3+ deep chains from each primary position
- Proven in live training with video evidence

### B-Game
- Secondary positions with 2+ deep chains
- Can recover to A-game from any position

### Weak Position Recovery
- From every non-primary position, can escape or transition to a primary position
- No "dead zones" on the map

## 3. Game Plan Creation
The student can create position-specific game plans.

### Opponent Preparation
- Analyse an opponent's likely game
- Identify positions to avoid
- Identify positions to target
- Create a chain of preferred transitions

### Competition Readiness
- Standing game plan (first 30 seconds)
- Preferred takedown → pass → finish chain
- Defensive contingency from each position
- Back-up plans for primary attacks failing

## 4. Teaching Capability
The student can explain and teach.

### Position Explanation
- Can explain any position on the map with correct terminology
- Can demonstrate the key techniques for each position
- Can identify and correct common errors

### System Explanation
- Can explain the logic of a system (not just the moves)
- Can show why chains work (opponent reactions drive branches)
- Can adapt explanations to different experience levels

## 5. Live Proof Requirements

### Sparring Evidence
- Video of successful A-game execution against skilled opponents
- Video of recovery from worst positions
- Video of chain execution (3+ moves) in live sparring
- Video of scramble resolution

### Competition Evidence (recommended, not mandatory)
- Competition footage showing game plan execution
- Or equivalent high-level sparring footage reviewed by Aaron
