## Blue Belt — Second Layer

### Blue Belt Guard / Bottom Direction
The guard section expands from White belt's 2 positions to 5 positions with deeper content:

#### Layer 1: Deepen White Belt Guards
- **Closed Guard (A):** Add guard opening defence (passer side awareness). Flag: guard player sweeps/additional subs not yet mapped in OPML -- needs Aaron to add content before picks.
- **Knee Shield Half Guard (B):** Add passer offence perspective (how to pass the knee shield). Mirror awareness: student learns both sides.

#### Layer 2: New Guard Positions
- **Butterfly Guard (C):** First new open guard. Passer offence (bodylock/high tripod/shoulder crunch) + guard player defence (shoulder crunch prevention). Flag: guard player sweeps/subs not yet mapped.
- **Half Butterfly (D):** Second new open guard. Passer offence (high tripod/bodylock). Flag: guard player side sparse in OPML.
- **Supine Guard (E):** Guard player depth -- White belt only covered passer side. Adds counter-defences (coil, frames, K guard, inverting) + control (camping control points).

#### Design Principles
1. White belt guards teach "what to do from here." Blue belt adds "what to do when they counter."
2. New positions chosen because they appear frequently in no-gi and have mapped content.
3. Guard player offence (sweeps/subs) from Butterfly/Half Butterfly/Closed Guard is OPML-limited -- Aaron needs to map that content before it can be picked.
4. Every pick must come from Aaron's existing mapped content. No invented techniques.
