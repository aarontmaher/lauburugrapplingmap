If it’s saved from your phone and you’re trying to use it from your laptop via iCloud, the key question is where it was saved:

If it was saved to Photos on your phone

On your laptop, it will show up in the Photos app, not as a normal file in Finder.

So:
	•	open Photos on the Mac
	•	find the video there
	•	export it or drag it out if you need a normal file

This only works if iCloud Photos is turned on for both phone and laptop.

If it was saved to Files / iCloud Drive on your phone

On your laptop, it will show up in Finder → iCloud Drive as a normal file.

So:
	•	open Finder
	•	click iCloud Drive
	•	find the folder you saved it into on the phone
	•	download it locally if needed

Why you may not be seeing it

Usually one of these is happening:
	•	you saved it to Photos, but you’re looking in Finder
	•	you saved it to Files, but you’re looking in Photos
	•	iCloud sync hasn’t finished yet
	•	the file is still only a cloud placeholder and hasn’t downloaded on the Mac yet
	•	you saved it into On My iPhone, not iCloud Drive

Best way to check on the phone

On iPhone:
	•	open the video
	•	tap Share
	•	see whether it says it’s in Photos or Files
	•	if in Files, check the exact folder path

Best workflow for CapCut

If you want to import it into CapCut on the laptop, the easiest path is:
	•	save it into iCloud Drive
	•	then on the laptop open Finder → iCloud Drive
	•	drag that file into CapCut

If it’s only in Photos, then:
	•	open Photos on the Mac
	•	drag/export the clip out first
	•	then import into CapCut
